Bhagat Singh

About:

Bhagat Singh (born September 27/28, 1907, Banga, Lyallpur, western Punjab, India [now Faisalabad, Pakistan]—died March 23, 1931, Lahore [now in Pakistan]) was an Indian freedom fighter who was honored as a hero of the Indian Independence Movement after his execution by British authorities at the age of 23. He is also known as Shaheed Bhagat Singh (shaheed meaning “martyr”).

Early Life:

Singh was born into a Jat Sikh family to Kishan Singh and Vidyavati. The exact date of his birth remains a topic of debate, with sources differing between September 27 and September 28, 1907. His desire to fight against the British was inspired by his family’s involvement in the freedom struggle, in which his grandfather, father, and uncles actively participated.

Contribution:

He played a major role in the Indian independence movement. His actions and bravery made him a national hero.
